# spec.md — Realm

## Product concept
A self-contained, text-based fantasy-kingdom management game in the spirit of
Crusader Kings / Football Manager. You are a hereditary ruler. You set taxes,
build the realm, manage your dynasty, conduct diplomacy, and wage war. Every
character, event, price, and war outcome is **procedurally generated by the
simulation** — no network, no APIs, no external datasets. Python 3 stdlib only.

The win/lose frame: keep your **dynasty on the throne**. The game ends when your
bloodline dies out with no heir (a succession crisis), or you abdicate.

## The player's core loop (a single turn = one year)
Each turn runs these phases in order (see `architecture.md` for the data flow):

1. **Economy** — collect tax income, pay army upkeep; tax pressure & debt feed
   back into stability; prosperity trends toward stability; population drifts.
2. **Event** — one procedurally-filled event with 2–3 choices, each with a
   distinct mechanical effect.
3. **Decree** — the player's deliberate action: taxes, invest, recruit, feast,
   diplomacy (gifts), marriage alliance, declare war, or hold court.
4. **War** — resolve any active wars (and possible invasions by hostile, much
   stronger neighbours) with win/lose consequences.
5. **Aging & succession** — everyone ages; a married ruler may gain an heir;
   mortality rises with age; a ruler's death resolves succession to exactly one
   heir or a defined crisis (game over).

## Systems
- **Treasury** — integer gold; income = f(population, tax_rate, prosperity,
  ruler stewardship); upkeep = f(army). May go negative (debt) with penalties.
- **Succession** — eldest living child → eldest living sibling → crisis. New
  ruler costs stability/legitimacy.
- **War** — power = army × ruler-martial × stability vs neighbour strength × RNG.
  Win → plunder, legitimacy. Lose → troop losses, indemnity, instability, small
  chance the ruler dies in battle.
- **Diplomacy** — each neighbour has a relation (−100..100); gifts and marriages
  raise it; war and refusals lower it; very hostile + stronger neighbours invade.
- **Events** — 6 templates (plague, plot, harvest, sellsword, unrest, envoy),
  filled with generated names/places; each option mutates state.

## Acceptance criteria (self-verifiable — see `test_kingdom.py`)
Each criterion is an automated test the simulation passes/fails with no human
judgment. `python test_kingdom.py` prints PASS/FAIL.

- [x] **A1** Self-play harness runs a full 30-turn reign for 10 seeds with zero
  crashes and zero invariant violations. (Exit condition also requires this to
  hold; harness: `python kingdom.py --selfplay 10 30`.)
- [x] **A2** Treasury is always an integer; debt (negative treasury) is
  reachable over 20 seeds.
- [x] **A3** Killing the ruler from any reachable state always resolves to
  exactly one living ruler, or a defined crisis carrying a reason.
- [x] **A4** No dead choices: every event option and every decree option moves a
  healthy realm's state (167 option-checks).
- [x] **A5** War produces both victories and defeats with real consequences over
  40 seeds.
- [x] **A6** Diplomacy: the "send gifts" decree measurably raises a neighbour's
  relation.
- [x] **A7** All bounded stats (prosperity, stability, legitimacy, tax_rate)
  stay within [0,100] across 50 turns × 10 seeds.
- [x] **A8** No event template fires twice in a row within a reign.
- [x] **A9** A neighbour just warred cannot be re-declared on until its cooldown
  clears (then becomes available again).
- [x] **A10** Debt penalty scales with depth: deeper debt costs more stability.
- [x] **A11** Prestige score is a non-negative integer that grows as a reign
  endures (shown at game over / abdication).
- [x] **A12** An unmarried ruler can take a spouse (so the line can continue).
- [x] **A13** The game never offers to marry away your last heir; the alliance
  marriage appears only when a spare heir remains.
- [x] **A14** A "keep the line going" policy survives most 60-year runs (ruler
  marriage makes a young, childless heir no longer a dead-end).
- [x] **A15** Peak stability erodes without upkeep — a contented realm is not a
  frictionless attractor; holding it high costs active decisions.

## Out of scope (deliberately, per ponytail/YAGNI)
- Save/load, GUI, multiplayer, mod support, content beyond the 6 event templates.
  Add only if a concrete need appears in `ux_review.md`.
